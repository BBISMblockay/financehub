# Onboarding a new employee into SILO

Plain-language walkthrough for getting a new teammate set up in SILO. No tech background needed.

---

## Part 1 — What the admin does (Blake or another admin)

1. **Tell the new employee to go to the SILO login page** and click **"Create account."**
   They'll enter their name, work email, and a password (at least 6 characters). This just creates their login — it does **not** give them access yet.

2. **Approve their access.**
   - Sign in to SILO and open **Backend Hub** from the sidebar.
   - Go to the **Access Requests** section — you'll see their name and email waiting there.
   - Click **Approve**, then set:
     - **Department** — which team they're on (e.g. Finance, Marketing, Ops, Retail, Exec)
     - **Role** — almost everyone should be **Admin** (that's our standard access level)
   - Click confirm/save.

3. **Let them know they're approved** — they can now sign in.

If you don't see their request, double check they actually completed the "Create account" step (not just tried to sign in).

---

## Part 2 — What the new employee does

### Step 1: Create your account
1. Go to the SILO login page (your manager will send you the link).
2. Click **"Create account."**
3. Fill in your name, your **@baseballism.com** email, and pick a password.
4. Click **Create account**.

You'll see a message saying your account was created. **You can't get in yet** — someone on the team needs to approve your access first (usually within a day).

### Step 2: Sign in
1. Once you hear you've been approved, go back to the login page.
2. Enter your email and password.
3. Click **Sign in**.

You'll be taken straight to your home screen — SILO automatically sends you to the right place based on your department.

*(If you ever forget your password, click "Forgot password?" on the login screen and follow the email link.)*

### Step 3: Get familiar with the layout
- The **left sidebar** is your main menu — it only shows the tools your department actually uses, so don't worry about a cluttered list.
- The top of most pages has a **title and breadcrumb** so you always know what page you're on.
- Numbers and data tables use a monospace font (looks like a receipt) — that's normal, it's just for readability.

### Step 4: Set up your profile
1. In the sidebar, find **My Profile**.
2. Confirm your name and department look right.
3. Set your **default page** if you want SILO to open somewhere specific every time you log in.

### Step 5: Know where to go for your job
Depending on your department, here's what you'll use most:

| Department | What you'll mostly use |
|---|---|
| Finance | Cash flow, Revenue Projections, Payment Requests |
| Purchasing | PO Builder, PO Landed Cost, Factories |
| Marketing | Launch Calendar, Task Manager |
| Retail / Ops | Request Manager, Inventory Manager, Product Tracker |
| Exec | Full dashboard / Executive overview |

If you don't see a tool you think you need, ask your manager — access can be adjusted by an admin in Backend Hub.

### Step 6: The basics to remember
- **Don't share your password** or your login with anyone else, even a teammate.
- **Don't use "alert" popups or browser tricks** to try to fix errors — if something looks broken, take a screenshot and send it to Blake or whoever manages SILO.
- Everything you do in SILO (payment requests, POs, launches) is tied to your name — it's how we track who did what.
- If you switch computers or clear your browser data, you'll just need to sign in again — nothing is lost.

### Step 7: Who to ask for help
- Access problems (can't log in, wrong department shown) → the admin who approved you (Blake, blake@baseballism.com)
- "This page looks wrong" or a data question → whoever owns that tool on your team
- Anything urgent/blocking → message Blake directly

## Quick checklist (print or copy into onboarding docs)

**Admin side:**
- [ ] New employee told to create account at login page
- [ ] Access request approved in Backend Hub (department + role set)
- [ ] Employee confirmed they can sign in

**Employee side:**
- [ ] Created account
- [ ] Signed in successfully
- [ ] Reviewed My Profile
- [ ] Knows which tools apply to their department
- [ ] Knows who to contact for help
