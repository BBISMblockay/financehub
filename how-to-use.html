User guide

How to use SILO

SILO is your company’s internal web hub: finance, inventory, purchasing, and more. This page walks through signing in, where you land, and how to get around—without technical jargon.

Getting started

  1. Ask your admin for a SILO account (they create your profile and, if needed, send a Supabase invite email).
  2. Bookmark your company’s SILO URL (the site root and /pages/login.html both work).
  3. Open the login page, sign in, and wait for the automatic redirect into your department hub or home tool.
Tip: Many tools open inside an embedded frame in your hub. If something looks cramped, use Open in new tab (where available) for a full-width window.

Signing in

SILO uses email-based sign-in backed by Supabase. On the login page you can switch between:

  • Password — enter the email and password you set (or that you chose after an invite).
  • Magic link — enter your email; SILO emails you a one-time link. Check spam, and complete email confirmation first if your org requires it.

If you need an account, use Create account on the login page only when your team has enabled self-sign-up; otherwise ask an admin.

Invites, passwords, and magic links

Invite or “reset password” from email

If your link contains a long fragment in the address bar (often type=invite, recovery, or magiclink), open it normally. The site’s entry page (/index.html) detects that and sends you to /pages/set-password.html to finish setup before you use the rest of SILO.

After setup

Once your password is saved (or magic link session is active), sign in again from the login page if prompted. You should then be routed into the right area for your role.

Where you land after login

After a successful sign-in, SILO looks up your profile (role and department) and sends you to a default home. Admins can also set a custom default page on your profile.

Your profile Typical first screen
Owner, or Finance / Exec department /finance.html (Finance hub)
Operations department /ops.html (Ops hub)
Retail department /retail.html (Retail hub)
Marketing department /marketing.html (Marketing hub)
Other departments Usually /finance.html or the fallback set in pages/config.js — ask your admin if unsure.
Home from the site root: visiting /index.html while signed in runs the same routing logic and forwards you to the right hub automatically.

Department hubs (sidebar & tools)

Hubs such as Finance, Ops, Retail, and Marketing share the same idea: a sidebar lists approved tools; the main area shows the selected tool. You stay signed in across tools in that session.

  • Search (Finance) — filters the menu when you have many links.
  • Reload — refreshes the current tool (useful after saving data in a form).
  • Open in new tab — opens the current tool in a separate browser tab.
  • Sign out — ends your session; use this on shared computers.

Some menu items open external sites (for example Jotform grids or Power BI). Those may show an “External” label—your browser may ask to allow pop-ups or third-party cookies for those sites.

Standalone pages worth bookmarking

Not every tool lives in a hub menu. Your team may share direct links for specialized workflows, for example:

  • /pages/sales-verification.html — sales verification workflows
  • /pages/purchase-request.html — purchase requests
  • /pages/request-manager.html — reviewing payment / request queues
  • /pages/launch-calendar.html — detailed launch calendar (when not opened from a hub)

If a link shows a blank screen or “no access,” your profile may not include permission for that table—ask an admin to adjust your role or department.

When something goes wrong

  • “Account is inactive” — your login exists but profiles.is_active is off. Contact an admin to activate you.
  • Stuck at login after success — try a hard refresh, or clear site data for SILO, then sign in again.
  • Embedded tool is blank — try Open in new tab, disable strict blockers for that session, or check whether the external site requires a separate login.
  • Wrong department / wrong home page — ask an admin to update your department or default page on your profile.
  • Data looks stale — many numbers come from scheduled syncs or spreadsheets; ask finance or ops when the last refresh ran (see technical overview for pipelines).
Who to ask: for access and profile fixes, your SILO administrator or IT contact. For business process questions (what to enter in a form), use your team’s SOP or manager.
